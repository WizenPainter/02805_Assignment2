==The Wilder family== ==\n'''[[Geoffrey Wilder|Geoffrey]]''' and '''Catherine Wilder''' (\"the Thieves\") are [[Alex Wilder]]'s parents. They are [[Boss (crime)|crime bosses]] who posed as [[Businessperson|businesspeople]]. They handled [[illegal drug trade]], [[gambling]] and [[robbing|robbery]] in Los Angeles. For this, these two established strong and powerful connections throughout Los Angeles, a reason the Pride is able to frame their kids for kidnap and murder. Geoffrey had led the entire Pride.<ref name=\"Vol1#17\">[http://runaways.mergingminds.org/showarticle.asp?section=10&fldAuto=410 Runaways: Vol. 1, #17] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080728054532/http://runaways.mergingminds.org/showarticle.asp?section=10&fldAuto=410 |date=2008-07-28 }}</ref> Geoffrey originally appeared as a stern and demeaning man, who had forbade his son from playing online roleplaying games.<ref name=\"Vol1#1\">''Runaways'' #1</ref> His cool persona is often distinguished, meaning other characters can detect his attitude, as evidenced when Nico is able to identify a 1985 version of Geoffrey. When the Gibborim had called them to the first meeting, Geoffrey and Catherine had been on the run from the cops, after a fresh robbery,<ref name=\"Vol1#13\">''Runaways'' #13</ref> where it was revealed that Geoffrey and Catherine had [[elope]]d, defying Catherine's mother's wishes.<ref name=\"Vol1#13\"/> Originally appearing as average parents, it is shown the two can also have short tempers; when one of the Pride's agents informed Geoffrey that their children had run away, Geoffrey carried a dagger to Alex's room; Catherine later shot the same agent in the knee.<ref name=\"Vol1#10\">''Runaways'' #10</ref>\n\nGeoffrey Wilder returns, after a group unintentionally brings a 1985 version of him back from his time.<ref name=\"Vol2#14\">''Runaways'' vol. 2 #14</ref> He was later defeated and returned to his time with his memory wiped by Nico.<ref name=\"Vol2#18\">''Runaways'' vol. 2 #18</ref>\n
